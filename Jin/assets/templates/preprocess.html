Data Pre-Processing

“Data pre-processing is one of the most important steps in machine learning. It helps in building machine learning models more accurately. In machine learning, there is an 80/20 rule. Every data scientist should spend 80% time for data pre-processing and 20% time to actually perform the analysis.” (1)

Pre-processing Data Process Flow.

The data flow diagram (DFD) illustrates the data gathering process and selection.

Correlation Matrix

The first visualization generated is a correlation matrix. The correlation matrix gives us an overview of correlations between factors within our data.

Good and Bad Loan .

The main goal of the project is to identify factors that contribute to a loan being successfully paid in full. The good loans and bad loans were sorted in order to compare the loan status against other factors.

The pie charts on the left show that longer term loans have a higher percent of bad loans. This information guides to a further exploration into how the term of a loan impacts the likelihood of the loan being paid off.

Loan Status by Loan Amount.

The visualizations below show the breakout of the average loan amount and interest rate of the loans by the status of the loan.
The data is also broken out to show the term of the loan. This offers a better understanding of the pie charts above by showing that shorter term loan amounts and interest rates are, on average, lower than longer term loan. Both of these factors most likely contribute to a higher percentage of short term loans being paid off.

Loan Amount and Interest Rate by Status.

This visualization display the average and data distribution of the loan amounts and interest rate.

Loan Amount by purpose.

Looking on how the purpose impacts the amount of the loan, and whether or not the loan is good or bad. It looks like loans that are awarded in large amounts are more difficult for borrowers to repay. Specifically, it appears that housing and small business loans are less successfully paid off than loans awarded for other purposes.

Loan Amount Applied vs Funded.

This visualization shows a comparison of the distribution of the amount applied for by the borrower and the amount awarded to the borrower. Both of these distributions are the same, which means that if a qualified borrower applies for a loan, they will most likely receive the amount they applied for in full.
